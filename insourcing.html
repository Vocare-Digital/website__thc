What is insourcing?

Insourcing is the practical use of NHS or HSE hospital facilities at times and in places where space and equipment are not being used to optimal levels. Our teams of clinical experts step into hospital departments, primarily across weekends - when they would otherwise be closed - and work closely with those departmental teams to offer the same processes and procedures.

Without the normal daily pressures, our teams are able to concentrate solely on the treatments in hand and work unencumbered to treat larger numbers of patients.

By using this spare capacity, Totally Healthcare can see many more patients than would otherwise be possible and thus, reduce waiting times.

Insourcing is highly cost-effective, and Totally Healthcare is able to treat patients in this manner at a fraction of the cost of sending patients out of their area for treatment.

How we do it

The following steps provide an overview of how Totally Healthcare will mobilise the service, working alongside hospitals to develop a bespoke service to meet individual requirements.

Identify Requirements

In order to provide a tailor-made solution to your waiting list issues, we will work with you to identify your requirements as well as exploring the reasons behind any backlogs.

Following these initial discussions, the team will put together a proposal for you to review, including how long an initiative is likely to take based on the facilities available

Groundwork

The team at Totally Healthcare know that groundwork is the key to a successful initiative, so once an initial plan has been agreed the team will come and meet with you at your unit.

At this meeting, a site visit of the facilities will be undertaken as well as a walkthrough of the patient pathway to ensure that the service delivered mirrors the local pathway.

Mobilisation

Once the groundwork has been carried out, a mobilisation plan will be put into place including an agreed go-live date.

All contract arrangements are finalised prior to go-live, to ensure a smooth transition.

As part of the mobilisation plan, all Consultant CVs will be approved by the hospital in advance, to ensure that the hospital is satisfied with the quality of Consultants provided.

Due to the extensive resource available, an initiative can be mobilised within 2 weeks.

Feedback and Shared Learning

Following each service delivery session, a meeting will take place (either face to face or via conference call), providing an opportunity for feedback and to make any adjustments for future service delivery.

Regular audit reports are produced for each hospital, sharing clinical outcomes and performance information along with patient feedback.

Upon conclusion of each contract, a detailed shared learning report will be produced for the hospital.
